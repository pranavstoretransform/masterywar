Initial Case File

CRIME REPORT

On a quiet evening in the old part of town, a scream echoed through a narrow alley behind an antique market. Minutes later, a passerby discovered the body of Elena Voss — a collector of rare artifacts known for her sharp eye and guarded private life.


Elena lay on the cobblestones beside an overturned crate. Next to her body was a remarkable object — a rare antique pocket watch, its golden casing smeared with blood. Early examination suggests the watch itself was used as the murder weapon.


Two very different motives begin to emerge: money from Elena's personal life and the mysterious value of the watch itself.

FIRST EVIDENCE

• Rare antique pocket watch found beside the body — golden casing smeared with blood, dented. Determined to be the murder weapon.


• The extremely valuable artifact was left at the scene — not taken by the killer.


• Overturned crate near the body suggests a struggle.


• Elena's phone found in her coat pocket — unlocked and active.


• The alley is behind the antique market — a location known to Elena's inner circle only.


• No witnesses to the attack. A single passerby arrived too late.

// STAGE 04 — MAJOR TWIST

Everything Changes

A new revelation has emerged from the investigation. It changes the direction of the case entirely.

⚠
THE REVELATION
The unregistered "Buyer" phone number has been traced. It was purchased with cash — but CCTV footage from the mobile shop has identified the buyer.
INVESTIGATION UPDATE — DIGITAL FORENSICS REPORT
The anonymous "Buyer" who lured Elena to the alley was Daniel Voss — her ex-husband.

Daniel created the identity of the mystery buyer, sending cryptic messages from a burner phone purchased in cash. He convinced Elena to meet him privately in the alley to discuss selling the watch — a meeting she had no reason to suspect was dangerous.

When she arrived, he attacked her, grabbing the object she had brought with her — the pocket watch — and using it to strike the fatal blow. Because Daniel had no interest in the watch at all, he left it at the scene.

To investigators, it would initially look like the crime was connected to the artifact. But in reality, the watch was only a distraction.
IMPLICATION A
Daniel's real motive had nothing to do with the watch. If Elena died, his court-ordered alimony payments would stop immediately — ending a financial burden he could no longer sustain.
IMPLICATION B
Daniel knew the antique market alley — he had been there many times during the marriage. He knew Elena would go there alone, and he knew no one would suspect him over a watch dispute.
IMPLICATION C
Victor Lang and Dr. Lydia Grant both observed that anyone who wanted the watch would never have used it as a weapon or left it behind. Only someone who wanted Elena dead — not the artifact — fits the profile.
IMPLICATION D
Marcus Hale had a partial alibi. Dr. Grant and Victor Lang both had strong alibis. Daniel Voss had no alibi at all for the critical window — and he was the only person whose financial problem was solved by Elena's death alone.
