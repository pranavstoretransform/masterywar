Initial Case File

You've been handed the initial case file. Study the crime scene, understand the circumstances, and take note of every suspect.

CRIME REPORT

On the morning of November 3rd, 2024, housekeeper Mrs. Perkins discovered Dr. Edmund Harrow slumped in his study chair at 7:42 AM. The door was bolted from the inside. A glass of port wine sat half-consumed on the desk.


Cause of death: Cardiac arrest. Toxicology report is pending — but the physician who examined the body noted the faint but distinct scent of digitalis on the victim's lips.


Dr. Harrow kept a private safe in the study. It was found open and empty.

FIRST EVIDENCE

• Half-consumed glass of port wine on the desk — no second glass found.


• Letter opener on the floor, not the desk — unusual for the meticulous Dr. Harrow.


• Victim's personal journal open on the desk, final entry dated Nov 2nd: "I can no longer trust anyone in this house."


• The study window latch was recently re-painted — who repainted it?

// STAGE 04 — MAJOR TWIST

Everything Changes

A new revelation has emerged. It changes the direction of the investigation entirely.

⚠
THE REVELATION
The toxicology report has come back. Dr. Harrow did NOT die from digitalis poisoning.
FORENSIC PATHOLOGY REPORT — NOVEMBER 5TH
Dr. Harrow died of a deliberate overdose of his own prescribed digitalis medication — administered in a dose five times the therapeutic threshold. However, the pathologist has confirmed that the port wine was not the delivery vehicle. The toxin was absorbed transdermally — through skin contact. The letter opener found on the floor had been coated with a concentrated digitalis solution. Dr. Harrow habitually used a letter opener — and whoever placed it on his desk that evening knew exactly what they were doing.

Furthermore: the safe was not emptied by the killer. A second key to the safe has been found in a kitchen drawer — registered to Nora Perkins. The safe's contents — a handwritten letter — were removed by her before she called the police.
IMPLICATION A
The killer needed access to the study to place the coated letter opener. The study was locked — but the window latch was freshly repainted, making it impossible to verify if it had been forced.
IMPLICATION B
Nora Perkins had a key to the safe. She removed the letter inside before police arrived. What did that letter say — and why was she so desperate to hide it?
IMPLICATION C
Digitalis in concentrated liquid form is not something a layperson manufactures easily. It requires either medical knowledge or access to a medical facility — like, for instance, Harrow & Culvert Clinic.
IMPLICATION D
Margaret Vale lied about her knowledge of digitalis. She had a prescription in 2022. She was facing removal from the will. She was in the house that evening with unsupervised access to every room.
